What it is?

The papermaking process consists of several main steps: stock preparation, sheet forming, pressing, drying, and surface finishing. Starch is an important component of many paper qualities. Starch consumption by weight in paper making and paper conversion processes ranks third after cellulose fiber and mineral pigments. Starch is used as a flocculant and retention aid, as a binding agent, as a surface sizing agent, as a binder for coatings, and as an adhesive in corrugated board, laminate grades, and other products. The main sources of starch are corn, potatoes, waxy corn, wheat, and tapioca. Processed starch is available as powder or slightly aggregated pearl starch. Unmodified (native) starch is rarely used in the paper industry, except as a binder in laminations and rolling processes. Most starches used in papermaking are specialty products that have been modified through hydrolysis, oxidation, or controlled derivatization.
